Clam chowder

To make the clam chowder, we started by producing consistent cuts of macedoine mirepoix, potato, bacon, and pork loin, the latter two cut on a red board and fried off in a frying pan to cook through before being set aside ready as a garnish.

In another pan, we lightly fried the mirepoix then added the potatoes, then white wine, fish stock made ready that day, and clam meat.

To thicken this soup, we finished in the traditional American way, using a water biscuit broken up into the soup.

This absorbs liquid from the soup and becomes soaked through, breaking down into a mush which can be completely combined with the soup as a thickener.

To this, we added orange juice and cream and garnished with a tomato concasse and chopped parsley.
