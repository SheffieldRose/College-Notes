French stick

Mix this dough quickly for 3 minutes and slowly for 10. Flour used in this is 13.5% protein – no ascorbic acid – unbleached. The protein content is important because it tells you how much water you can get into the bread (which creates bubbles during the autolyse process).

The rising process is also created as the starch in the flour absorbs water and creates steam. Damaged starch absorbs a lot more water than normal starch which explains why the heating process helps.

To make this, we mixed 1kg bread flour (Grand Siècle) with 750g water covered and left it for 30 minutes to undergo the autolyse process.

Once this was done, we added 18g salt and 10g yeast and mixed for 10 minutes on the first speed and 3 minutes on the second speed then left to ferment for 30 minutes.

We folded it, split into 320g sized-pieces and left it in the fridge overnight. Finally, we baked it at 250°c for 20 minutes.
