Paupiettes de truite

Create a fleuron out of well-rested puff pastry and an egg wash and sesame, cut int a crescent moon-shape.

Fish Mousse (Farce):

  • 50g salmon – puree and pass through a tamie – tamiser
  • 50g cream (double – so 42%-48%)
  • 1 tsp egg beaten (The egg binds the protein in the salmon and helps it coagulate and that means that the fat from the cream can go into the fat from the salmon)
  • (nutmeg, salt, pepper)

Put the farce onto the truite fillets then flatten/smooth down.

Roll and then wrap roll in greaseproof paper then roll in foil to keep shape.

Place into the buttered sauterse and put the paupiette into it and cook.

In a pan make a fish stock. Sweat down under a cartouche for 20 minutes, (no more) into a stock:

  • Leek
  • Lemon (a few drops)
  • Parsley
  • Shallot
  • Onion
  • Celery
  • Mushrooms (not necessary)
  • Fish bones (white fish)
  • White wine – 100ml
  • Cook in a fully buttered sauterse

Debeard mussels and drop into a pan that’s frying shallots and add a splash of white wine and a splash of fish stock.

Reduce fish and mussel stock and add 150ml double cream and mount with cold diced butter then “vanner”. Add trout juice and pass off.

Add mussels to sauce and “naper” Fry button mushrooms Fry a couple of prawns for service Add florons and serve all together.
