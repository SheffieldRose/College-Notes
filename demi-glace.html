Demi-glace

To make this, we first made a mirepoix from chopped leeks, carrots, celery and onions and fried them off while roasting some beef bones in the oven to set off a Maillard reaction, ready for making the stock.

Once the bones had come to a dark brown colour, we put them in the stock pot with the mirepoix and covered with water to simmer away and stew the flavours out of the ingredients.

To achieve a demi-glace, we cooked this for at least eight hours, reducing the liquid in the pot to intensify the taste.

This now will be stored in the freezer for future use and used as the base for many other stocks and sauces.
