Trussing

This term refers to tying a bird in such a way that it cooks according to the desired manner. For example, you can truss for:

  • roast (tying the legs and wings in tightly to the body with string to make sure the bird cooks evenly during cooking)
  • entrée (pulling the meat from the thigh bone and making a slit in the skin through which the thigh bone is reintroduced, turning the thigh into a dome shape with the thigh bone poking out the top)
  • relève (a joint or butchers cut to be sliced for the main part of the meals)
