Notes

Sushi grade fish is frozen to kill any parasites (with the added bonus that tempering it makes the fish easier to cut.)

A cross-section of lemon, in a slice with no rind is to be squeezed over the dish by the waiter using tongs.

A wedge of lemon is to be served for the customer for them to squeeze over the dish themselves.

Farmed fish in close proximity to each other can get damaged and lead to infection whether bacterial or fungal.

Glace is a fully reduced fish stock. Without gelatine it is a fumée (double strength fish stock) or an essence.

Oily fish bones contain oil and are bad for stock, furthermore, cod bones make fish stock go grey and should be avoided if you want a nice, clear stock.

Glazed and unglazed dishes are how fish dishes are split.

Farce is a fish puree with cream whereas a mousseline has twice the amount of cream and has a lighter texture.

In a white fish the oil is in the liver – in an oily fish the oil is in the flesh.

All oily fish are round, although white fish can be round or flat.

Ammonia, is a by-product of fish breaking down – this is all fish.

Cuts of fish:

  • Suprême
  • Darne
  • Délice
  • Paupiettes

Sea slime – this refers to the mucus excreted from the central nervous system of the fish which acts as a natural protective barrier. It’s a mild indicator of the freshness of the fish provided that it smells fresh and is clean and clear. If there is no “slime”, the fish may have been washed or may be older than expected as the mucus will as it will start to break down after time meaning other indicators of freshness such as bright gills and eyes should be referred to.
