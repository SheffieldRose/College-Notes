Darne de saumon chambord

To get the darne of salmon, I cut a cross-section of the salmon through its spine and belly and put into a fully buttered a sauteuse pan and covered half with red wine and fish stock.

In the meantime, I poached some quenelles of fish mousse which I made from a mix of pureed fish, an egg, a reasonable amount of salt and double cream (The salt tightens the proteins in the fish to help it keep together during the cooking process. The egg contains even protein in the albumen for firming and lecithin in the yolk which helps emulsify the fats in the fish and the cream, also aiding the bind).

Once poached, I lightly fried for colour.

At the same time, I glazed some shallots in sugar and red wine, pan-fried some button mushrooms and in turn some floured milt ready for serving.

With all these components ready, I quickly deep fried some plaice goujons as a garnish and served all together. with a sprinkle of chopped chives on top.
