Canapé Méphisto

To start with, we made barquettes of very thin, delicate short pastry and left them to cool. To make this dough, we used a recipe that called for 1 kg plain flour, 250g butter, 5 egg yolks, and 250ml of water.
Then we made a quick sauce diable from a white wine vinegar, shallot reduction which we then bulkled out with a tomato-meat demi-glace.
Once the sauce was ready, we pan-fried plaice milt agitating the pan so that the sack rolled into a spiral shape and glazed it with the sauce.
The milt was then placed into the barquette and garnished with a red chilli brunoir.
