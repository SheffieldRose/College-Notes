Cutting a bird for sauter

This term refers to the cut of 8 pieces that can be taken off a chicken - 4 white pieces and 4 brown pieces. and can be divided into a white sauter and a brown sauter.

  1. Remove the parson’s nose
  2. Take out the wishbone
  3. Remove the wing tips
  4. Remove the wing
  5. Remove the legs and oysters
  6. Slice down between the middle of the cavity with the entire crown on top
  7. Cut it in half horizontally
  8. Cut the feet from the legs
  9. Separate the drumstick and the thigh bone
  10. Truss thigh bones for entrée ( by pulling the meat from the thigh bone and making a slit in the skin through which the thigh bone is reintroduced, turning the thigh into a dome shape with the thigh bone poking out the top)
