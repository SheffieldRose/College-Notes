Filleting a trout

  1. Take the head off by slicing just behind the gills and cutting downwards all the way
  2. Remove the guts by pulling them through the hole just made
  3. Descale the fish using the back of a knife against the flow of the scales and wipe down to remove anything that sticks after removal
  4. Depin using tweezers and gently tugging the pins out while pressing onto the flesh to avoid damaging the flesh. If there is a large amount of ribs after removing fillet from the ribcage, using a knife to slice just underneath them also works and is a clean way of removing bones
  5. Trim the belly fat from the bottom of the fillet
  6. Dip fingers in salt to provide grip when handling the fillet and use a filleting knife to cut into the tail-end of the fish down to the skin then along the skin (between the skin and the flesh) toward the head-end of the fish
