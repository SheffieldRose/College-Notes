Notes

  • Autolyse refers to the period after mixing water and flour, where a dough is left to rest before fermenting with yeast. This makes sure that the dough is nicely hydrated, "waking up" the enzymes in the proteins and glucose, ready for action. As a result, the kneading time reduces (reducing air contact therefore reducing oxidisation).
  • Kneading aligns the glutenin and gliadin (protein classes) into disulfide bonds which make up gluten , organising the bond into a net-like structure which traps the carbon dioxide produced by the yeast and helps the dough rise.
