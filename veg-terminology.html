Vegetable terminology

  • Chiffonade - is thin strips of leaves like mint, sorrel, lettuce etc.
  • Game chips - waffle shaped chips achieved using fluted attachment on a mandolin, served with game
  • Macedoine - small dices of vegetables
  • Maris Pipers - low starch content so they don't brown quickly
  • Matignon - is so that flavour can disperse in the dish to be passed out
  • Mirepoix - is a matignon to be served in the dish
  • Parmentier - dice-sized cubes of potato
  • Paysanne - a cut of thin shapes, such as squares or triangles mostly for garnishing
  • Pommes allumettes - matchstick chips
  • Pommes batailles - parmentier potatoes, deep fried
  • Pommes frites - regular thin chips
  • Pommes gaufrettes - "waffle chips"/game chips (for other root veg, dehydrate them slightly first)
  • Pommes mignonés - short chips
  • Pommes pailles - straw chips, or julienne chips
  • Pommes pont neufs - bridge-shaped chips

Orange zest used in some of our garnishes contains the right components to make pectin, a useful setting agent. It can also be used in wine finings.
