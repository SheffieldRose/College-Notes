Brown stock

Brown stocks take considerably longer to achieve than a white or fish stock – taking around 8 hours to cook. The difference in technique here is that the bones are roasted off first, rather than blanched, in order to get Maynard reaction started (at around 50-150°c) to impart a deeper flavour and colour into the stock.

To take it to stock browning colour (dark caramel), you would need to cook it at around 172°c. Typical brown stocks may be made from veal or beef etc.

An example being estaphade, made from beef and pork.
