Velouté Agnès Sorrel

To make this, you start with whisking a light brown roux (the colour of café au lait) of 50g plain flour and 50g melted butter in a pan, which we did to start our Velouté Agnès Sorrel.

We then raised the volume of the pan with 1 litre of chicken stock, whisking all the time to ensure there were no lumps. We then baked the velouté on the boiling plate of the stove, at a constant an medium heat, covered with a cartouche and lid to avoid evaporation or the formation of a skin of top of the soup.

Once the flour had cooked out and the process of gelatinisation of the starch had ended, we finished the soup off with a “glaçage” or “liaison”, which is two parts whipped cream to one part egg yolk (in this case, 50g whipped cream to one egg yolk).

This glaçage smoothens out and slightly thickens the soup to the desired consistency. This, we then topped with a julienne of mushroom, chicken and beef tongue.
