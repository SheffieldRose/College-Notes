Jugged hare

Jugged hare is normally marinated for 7 days before cooking (which is assuming that the game is left to become “high” or ripe for cooking.

The technique of jugging is one that involves stewing a whole animal for a long time in an earthenware jug or pot which is stood in a pan of boiling water. This will be thickened with the blood of the animal towards the end of the cooking process – also known in French as a civet.

Coat hare with flour and fry in a shallow pan.

Chop a very neat Matignon bordelaise as it will be served with the dish.Mix, fry and cover with red wine

Take shallots and take off root ends but leave the skin on and blanch for three minutes. Peel, and then caramelise.

Fry off button mushrooms

Blanch some lardons of bacon to stiffen them up then fry

Fry heart-shaped croutons in very hot butter then season.**

Sausage meatballs to garnish.

*Sippets are small pieces of bread that are toasted **Croutons are small pieces of bread that are fried
