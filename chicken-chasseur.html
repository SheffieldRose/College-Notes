Sauté chicken chasseur

First, we cut a chicken for sauté by removing the parson’s nose, taking the wishbone out, removing the wing tips, removing the wing, removing the legs and oyster, slicing down between the middle of the cavity with the entire crown on top, then cutting it in half horizontally.

Then we cut the feet from the legs and separated the drumstick and the thigh bone.

The carcass and winglets went to be frozen for future stock production and the sauté pieces were neatened up by trussing them with their own meat.

These pieces were then coated in flour and deep fried in a sauteuse with enough oil to fill the pan half-way. We fried these in oil that had been brought to a blue haze and once the pieces were golden brown, put them in the oven to cook all the way through and become juicy and tender.

In a different pan, while the chicken was cooking, we made the chasseur sauce by frying finely diced shallots and reducing with white wine.

Once reduced, we added a demi-glace, sliced mushrooms and a tomato concasse.

We served this all together in one sharing dish with chopped parsley to garnish.
