Roast pheasant

The farce à gratin is made from a fried off mix of garlic, onion, livers, sage and thyme. These are then flambed in brandy and mixed with double cream, breadcrumbs and chives.

After plucking, eviscerating and trussing the pheasant, then we fully coated the pheasant in butter and roasted in the oven on top of a mirepoix.

Once this had come out of the oven, we placed the bird on a sockle made from a hollowed and fried piece of bread that had been stuffed with the farce à gratin.

We finished the pheasant off with a beurre noisette and a squeeze of lemon juice with watercress to garnish.

We then finished with a jus made from the liquid left at the bottom of the roasting tin and some meat reduction.
