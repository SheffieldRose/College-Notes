Suprême de volaille en papillote à l’ananas

To make this dish, we broke the chicken down as follows – we removed the wishbone and ends of the wings (including the flesh from around the base part of the wing, still attached to the bird) and put them in the fridge to be saved for stock.

Then raised the suprême from the carcass, which also went into the fridge away from anything that it could contaminate.

We removed the skin and snipped the sinew on the top side of the breast to ensure that it kept its shape during cooking.

We then made a filled-in figure of eight shape from non-stick paper and painted it with melted butter. We then butterflied the supreme and stuffed it with chilli, coriander and garlic to flavour.

Then we closed the butterfly and sealed the paper and cooked in the oven until 75°c.
