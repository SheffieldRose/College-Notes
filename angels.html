Angels on horseback

Doing the same as above, we made this with oysters rather than prunes.

Taking it in turns to ensure we all had chance to develop the skill, we shucked the fresh oysters from their shells and kept them in cold water before rolling into an appropriate shape for cooking and pinning through.

Again, we made sure to use the red board for the bacon and a blue board for the oysters to avoid cross contamination (which would translate to a professional kitchen should someone with a shellfish allergy walk in and an alternative needed to be offered).

This we served in a cleaned-out shell, sprinkled cayenne over them and garnished with micro-herbs.
