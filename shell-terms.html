Shellfish terminology

Acrolein may be produced in this scenario due to the nature of the cooking process and may lead to an acrid, bitter taste or smell so be careful in this case. It can be reduced by cold smoking is done at a lower temperature for longer. Food must be cooked or cured before this process though, to avoid food poisoning.


Shellfish can be divided into two classes: mobile or immobile. Mobile can be further divided into crustacean or mollusc, which humans eat regularly. Mollusc can be divided into arthropod (e.g. horseshoe crab) or cephlapod (ones that have a "foot" e.g. snails)
