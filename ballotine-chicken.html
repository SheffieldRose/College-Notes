Ballotine chicken with mushroom duxelles

To prepare the ballotine chicken, we boned out the thigh on a leg of chicken, leaving the drumstick knuckle in the end by scraping the flesh away from the bones of the leg and cutting. Once this was done, we refrigerated the meat while we made the duxelles to stuff the leg with.

To make the duxelles, we finely chopped shallots and onions and fried them, finishing them off with white wine, thyme, cream and breadcrumbs.

We then piped the duxelles into the chicken leg and wrapped tightly in poitrine fumée to shape the leg into a sausage shape, wrapped it in cling film and poached in hot water.

Meanwhile, we made a cream-based sauce Madeira with sweated-down shallots, Madeira wine and cream and finished it off with chives, tarragon and parsley.

Once the chicken was cooked, we fried it in a pan to give it colour and flavour then rested it briefly before plating.
