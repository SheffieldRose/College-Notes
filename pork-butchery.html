Pork butchery demonstration

This butchery demonstration was performed by the head of Owen Taylor, a local wholesale butchers that supplies restaurants in this area. These are notes taken during the demonstration.

  • To create a breeding pig takes from 1 year to 18 months, a gilt generally weighs around 70-80kg for this.
  • Female pigs generally have a smoother back end if it needs to be identified with boars having a rougher end where the testicles will have sat before slaughter. It is illegal to castrate before slaughter.
  • The butcher performing this demonstration preferred female pigs for the quality of the meat.
  • Fat – the millimetres of fat surrounding the meat helps to determine the grade of pork.
  • At slaughter, the tongue and offal is removed, so heads no longer come with them as a result of the discovery of vCJD (something that the butcher dangerously referred to as a government conspiracy intended to control farmers, but is actually a life threatening neurodegenerative disease, based on naturally occurring protein misfolds “prions” that can be transmitted and propagated through consumption, particularly of tissue containing these proteins e.g. brain and spinal tissue).
  • The spine is removed at slaughter because it turns green quickly.
  • The hock refers to the shin.
  • Gammon is hind leg meat whereas ham is cooked.
  • In poor quality meat the flesh is sloppy and a darker colour. This colour comes from a build up of lactic acid which causes the meat to degrade quicker.
  • The shoulder end is sweeter and more succulent.
  • 83-85kg pigs are about the right size for curing.
