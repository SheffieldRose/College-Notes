Coulibiac

Eastern European in origin this dish comprises baked fish which has been filleted, skinned, pinned, stuffed and marinated. The components of this dish, which we made individually were:

  • Duxelles mushroom
  • Cache de riz
  • Savoury crêpe
  • Trout fillet
  • Nori sheets

Duxelles mushroom
In a pan we put finely chopped shallot, chopped button mushrooms (fine but big enough to add a good texture), and a finely chopped small sprig of thyme, then fried it all lightly to induce a slight Maynard reaction on the vegetables. In order to finish the duxelles, we added a drop of cream, a drop of white wine and a pinch of finely chopped parsley and set aside to cool and then placed in the fridge until construction of the dish.

Cache de riz
This we made from braising rice before stuffing the fish. We finely chopped a shallot and quickly fried to begin the Maynard process, adding flavour to the pan. To this we added a handful of rice, two parts stock to water and a sprig of thyme. We then covered with a cartouche and a lid and braised this on the hot plate until cooked through and al dente, then added a fine brunoir of red and green bell pepper and chopped tarragon.

Savoury crêpe
To make this we combined these ingredients in a mixing bowl before frying in a shallow pan:
Finely chopped thyme
Two eggs
A drop of oil
Salt and pepper
A tablespoon of cream
Strong flour bit by bit until the mixture gained a thickness similar to double cream
Finely chopped thyme
Finely chopped chives

Trout fillets
To do this we broke the fish down in sections as follows:
Head off
Guts out
Descale with the back of a knife so as not to damage skin
Cut down length of spine just on either side of the spine
Pin using fish tweezers
Trim off fat from belly edge of fillet
Skin

Nori sheet
We used shop-bought sheets of nori seaweed to add texture and flavour to the dish.

Boiled egg
We put 8-10 eggs in boiled water until solidly cooked through, then grated.
N.B. the skin between the shell and flesh of an egg is called the epithelium.

Putting the coulibiac together - the steps we followed were as below:
Sandwich the grated egg and some duxelles between the two fish fillets.
Wrap the fillet sandwich tightly in nori.
Wrap this in the savoury herb crêpe.
Roll out puff pastry, leaving enough room for edges once fish roll is placed on top. Roll one edge of the puff pastry thinner than the others to create a delicate seal for the pastry roll when rolling and sealing the fish inside
Before placing the fish onto the pastry, create a layered bed of duxelles then rice, leaving room for it to go all the way around the fish
Wrap fish in the pastry/duxelles/rice layer and seal on thin edge with water.
Finish by wrapping in pastry layer cut into crosshatch shape and glaze with an egg wash, then bake until cooked through.
